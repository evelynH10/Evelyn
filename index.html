Re-Tote Campaing Design

Non-profit organization campaign

This campaign promotes sustainable habits by encouraging individuals to reduce plastic waste and transition to reusable tote bags. Participants are invited to sign a pledge to stop using plastic bags and recycle one in exchange for a free, eco-friendly tote—taking a small step toward a cleaner, greener future.
I designed the campaign's visual tools, including the logo, promotional materials, pledge forms, social media graphics, and tote bag artwork. Each element was created to be both impactful and approachable, reinforcing the message of sustainability and inspiring community involvement.

Helphood App Project

Mobile app website and advertising

Helphood is an app for the community to share tools and other neccesary stuff between each other. They can find things they want to share and borrow from their neighbors that might otherwise just be gathering dust unused. This helps the community complete household, garden, or other tasks around the house. One neighbor might borrow a shovel to complete a task in thier backyard saving him time and money from having to buy his own. Poster Design

Designed with Adobe Illustrator

These are posters for the city of Huntington Park promoting two activities visitors or residents can do in this community. They can either go to the local park which is a very big park with a playground and excercise equipment or go to the farmer's market for fresh fruits and other great food.

This bus ad promotes a mobile app designed to help neighbors share tools within their community. The clean, minimalist design illustrates people connecting and lending tools to one another thus highlighting how the app facilitates collaboration and resource-sharing to complete everyday tasks.

I designed this poster inspired by the designs and creations from graphic designer Louise Fili. I was inspired by her Italian Art Deco style. She uses cursive type often in her designs so I incorporated that in this poster as well as her Italian style.
